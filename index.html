Wing II — The Memory Vault

Remember When

Birthdays

Our 13th Birthday

I remember you were the only one from our class who showed up, and you even brought Rama and Yumna with you. Mesk was still a little baby back then. My hair was super curly, and it was the first time we ever wore makeup. It was also the first and only time we hosted a birthday party.

Birthdays

Your 14th Birthday

Rama planned a whole surprise party for you and asked all of us to reject your invitation so we could surprise you instead. I remember Malak and I showing up extra early and calling Rama to come open the door for us. Later, you told us you saw us from the window and thought we were Indians.

Birthdays

Our Shared Celebration

It’s extra fun sharing a birth month. We finally made it work when we went out together in October to celebrate our birthdays with a pistachio milk cake. We asked the lady to write “MLM” on it, only to later find out it’s actually a cigarette brand.

Eid

Wrong Guests

We asked if you’d be home so we could drop off the meat, but your dad was actually expecting other guests. We remember sitting in the car while another car parked right behind us. When we got out, they got out too, but it never crossed our minds that they might be your guests. When we knocked, your father and all your brothers opened the door, and we had the most awkward staring contest ever until Rama came and said, “Oh, it’s Malak and Mariam, let them in.”

Eid

2ara2eesh

During Eid season, all the sweet biscuits usually disappear first, and the only thing left is always the 2ara2eesh. Mama would usually pack some for us to take to school just so we could finish them. When Teta made them at home, she would make around 5–10 kilos. We still remember introducing you to them for the first time and how much you loved them. Now we associate them with you, and every Eid it feels like a tradition that we have to save some for you.

School

7arankash

I’m not even sure if you remember this, but one time you had golden berries with you at school and offered me some. You called them golden berries, and I told you they’re called 7arankash. You found the name so funny and kept repeating it the whole time.

School

Yumna

I remember when Rama was still in high school. Malak and I would go there after classes every day to wait for Mama, and Yumna would always be under the stairs waiting for Rama too. We would play with her, carry her around, and spin her. She was so tiny back then.

Hangouts

Safa Park

This is funny, but there was a time when my family used to go to Safa Park every Saturday. For some reason, everyone narrated their entire lives on the class group chat, so people somehow knew that. One time you texted me saying you were at Safa Park, and I just replied with “enjoy.” I knew you were hinting that we should meet up, but I also knew there was no chance we were going because it wasn’t a Saturday.

Hangouts

Our First

At that time, you always talked about how your mom encouraged you to hang out with people and didn’t focus too much on how long you stayed out. One day you said you were at the mall while your sisters were with their friends and you were alone, so Malak and I told you we could come. Aladdin had just come out at the time, and there was a huge cinema display everyone was taking pictures with. We kept trying to convince you to take pictures with us, but you refused. It was one of the many times we embarrassed you during our hangouts.

Your House

Trampoline

Honestly, one of our favorite things about your house is the trampoline. We remember jumping on it during your birthday and another time when we came over right after school. I was still in my uniform and felt so hot that I took off my PE shirt, and you gave me one of your jackets to wear.

Hangouts

Our Mall

We went to Circle Mall when it had just opened, and we even rode around in the Nesto cart. It’s probably the mall we visited the most, especially during COVID times. We would always play in the arcade, then go sit at a café and open the toys we bought with our tickets. We even got matching shirts from Cotton On there once. And we can’t forget the time we got scammed into buying blue tea from that Chinese place that later closed down. We really have so many memories in that mall.

Souvenirs

Your Cool Travels

I remember that even before we became super close, you brought us soap from one of your trips. We were so shocked because you were giving gifts to all your close friends, and somehow we were included too. It’s always so fun when you bring us souvenirs from your travels. I still remember the brass tortoise you got for Myrtle. It was so thoughtful. Your gifts are always unique and fun to explore, whether it’s the rock that looks cool on the inside or the painted wooden piece. That’s why our number one mission in Egypt was getting you as many little pieces and souvenirs from our trip as possible.

Car Rides

Our Personal Driver

One of the biggest milestones was definitely you learning how to drive. It unlocked a whole new level of feeling grown up. You drove us to so many places, and the car conversations always hit differently. For some reason, all the things I can’t think of during the hangout suddenly come to me in the car, and then I just can’t stop yapping.

Our Taste Tester

Foodie Leen

Remember when we made onigiri, stir-fried noodles, and fried rice for you and Yumna when you came over to our house? It’s always so fun having you try the food we make. Your reactions are always so good that they genuinely boost our confidence.
